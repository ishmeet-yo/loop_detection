Positioning / marketing

Use language like “self‑reflection”, “mental wellbeing companion”, “helps you notice patterns and habits”, not “treats depression/anxiety”.

Add a clear disclaimer: “This is not a medical device and is not a substitute for professional mental health care or diagnosis.”

No medical device licenses yet

India: outside CDSCO as long as you remain standalone wellness.

USA, Canada, UAE: can be treated as consumer wellness app if you avoid treatment/diagnosis claims.

You do not need FDA / Health Canada device approval, CDSCO device registration, or DHA telehealth licensing at this stage.

You still need strong privacy / terms

Region‑agnostic minimum:

Clear privacy policy and terms of use (in English, and optionally local languages for India/UAE).

Explicit user consent for storing their mental health data.

Explain where data is hosted, who can access it, how long it is kept, and how users can delete it.

Align with:

India’s DPDPA expectations around sensitive data.
​

US FTC guidance (no deceptive claims).
​

Canada’s PIPEDA principles.
​

UAE PDPL‑style rules (purpose limitation, security).
